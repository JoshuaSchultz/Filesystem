The Abrahamite Religions


Abrahamic religions are a common name for a number of religions originating in ancient, Semitic monotheistic tradition, primarily Judaism, Christianity and Islam. They are called so because they trace their tradition back to the patriarch Abraham. Less frequently mentioned are Bahá'í, Druserne and Rastarianism, but also these include Abraham among their spiritual ancestors. Also, mermaid and Sikhism are sometimes mentioned because of a certain community of ideas or relationships with the Jewish and Islamic tradition, but they are not considered abrahamish in the real sense, since the borrowed elements do not include the Abraham narratives.
